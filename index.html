Counting Methods

Voting Systems Explained

Different mathematical models produce vastly different outcomes from the same set of votes.

First-Past-The-Post

The candidate with the most votes wins — even without a majority. Simple, but can produce winners most people didn't vote for.

⚡ Fun Fact: In FPTP, a candidate can win with just 35% of votes if the opposition is split!

Ranked Choice Voting

Voters rank candidates by preference. If no one gets >50%, the last-place candidate is eliminated and their votes redistribute until a majority winner emerges.

⚡ Fun Fact: RCV eliminates the "spoiler effect" — you can vote your heart without wasting your vote!
Click "Run Simulation" to begin the ranked choice counting process.

Proportional Representation

Parties gain seats proportional to their vote share. This ensures that minority voices are represented and power is shared.

⚡ Fun Fact: Most European democracies use PR, which encourages multi-party coalition governments!
